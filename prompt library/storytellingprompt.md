# The Last Voicemail

The train smelled faintly of wet wool and burnt coffee. Mira sat by the fogged window, tracing circles in the condensation while the city blurred past in silver streaks. Across from her, an old man struggled to unwrap a peppermint from trembling fingers.

“Need help?” she asked softly.

He smiled. “My wife used to do it for me.”

Mira unwrapped it carefully and handed it back. The old man studied her face for a moment too long.

“You remind me of someone,” he murmured.

She laughed weakly. “I get that a lot.”

The overhead lights flickered. Rain tapped harder against the glass. Mira checked her phone again: 11 missed calls from Dad.

She turned it face down.

The old man cleared his throat. “You should answer while you still can.”

Something in his voice made her chest tighten.

When the train reached her stop, she stood, pulling her coat tighter. Her phone buzzed again — this time a voicemail notification.

Her father’s voice cracked through the speaker.

“Mira… sweetheart… your mother was asking for you before she—”
